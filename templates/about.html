About Me

Hi, I'm Kelsey Schuster, a recent PhD graduate and soon-to-be data engineer. Welcome to my personal website, where I describe my research projects in the field of theoretical chemistry, as well as some of the data science and programming projects I've been working on in my spare time.

I grew up in Wisconsin, where I learned to appreciate winter weather and became a devoted Green Bay Packers fan. I moved on to study chemistry and mathematics at Wheaton College in Illinois, graduating summa cum laude in May 2011. I had become interested in computer programming in college and decided to pursue a PhD in theoretical chemistry, which would allow me to study chemistry using computer simulations and mathematics. I moved out to Berkeley, California and worked with Prof. David Chandler on researcher in glassy physics. I graduated with my PhD from UC Berkeley in August 2016.

I'm currently preparing for my new adventure as a data engineer.
